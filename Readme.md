Welcome to this repository. This space is dedicated to the conceptual approach to physics, known in many academic institutions in the United States as **"Physics for Poets."**

### Purpose of the Repository

Throughout my teaching career, I have always advocated against the "black box" model of education. Understanding physics is not about memorizing formulas or performing mechanical calculations; it is about understanding the **structure** of the universe.

Throughout my career, I have always approached physics through the lens of absolute rigor, utilizing mathematics as the natural and most powerful language to decode the universe. However, a significant shift occurred when I joined Lone Star College in Texas. I was tasked with designing and teaching an elementary physics course specifically for students in the humanities and social sciences—a vibrant audience that is typically not exposed to rigorous scientific content.

Accepting this assignment was both a privilege and a daunting challenge. It forced me to strip away the comfort of equations and find new ways to communicate complex physical truths. This experience transformed my perspective, pushing me to emphasize **reasoning through concepts** and historical context. It proved that even without the scaffolding of calculus, the structural beauty of physics remains intact and accessible to all students, regardless of their academic background.

Thus, this repository serves as a bridge between the rigorous world of physical laws and the inquisitive mind of the humanist. Here, we don't just solve problems; we explore history, classical thought, and the inherent beauty of the laws that govern our reality—from Galileo's dialogues to the elegance of Newtonian mechanics.

### What You Will Find Here:

* **Conceptual Frameworks:** Materials designed to build a solid mental structure of physical laws.
* **Historical Context:** Anecdotes and quotes from the giants who paved the way, making science a human endeavor.
* **Structural Understanding:** Resources focused on the logic behind the phenomena, ensuring that students "see" the physics before they calculate it.

As I often tell my students: **physics is the poetry of reality, written in the language of logic.**
